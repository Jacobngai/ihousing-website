## Understanding the Two Pricing Models

### Commission-Based Management: The Traditional Approach

Commission-based property management has been the industry standard for years. The model is simple: your management company takes a percentage (typically 15-20%) of every booking they secure for your property.

On the surface, this seems fair—after all, they only get paid when you do. But the reality is more complex:

- **Your costs scale with your success**: The more your property earns, the more you pay in commissions. Success gets penalized.
- **No incentive for maximum revenue**: Once a booking is secured, there's no financial motivation for the manager to optimize pricing or secure premium rates.
- **Unpredictable expenses**: You can never predict exactly how much you'll pay each month, making financial planning difficult.
- **Hidden costs**: Many commission models exclude certain services, adding surprise fees for cleaning, maintenance coordination, or guest support.

### Flat Fee Management: The Modern Alternative

Flat fee property management, like what iHousing offers, is exactly what it sounds like: you pay a fixed monthly rate regardless of how much revenue your property generates.

For property owners in Melaka, this means paying just **RM250 per month** for comprehensive management services—no commissions, no surprises.

- **Aligned incentives**: Your success is our success. The more you earn, the happier you are—and we want you to stay happy.
- **Complete transparency**: One flat price covers everything we do, with no hidden fees.
