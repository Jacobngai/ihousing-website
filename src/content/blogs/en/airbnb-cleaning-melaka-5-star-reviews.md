## The "Melaka Standard": Why DIY Cleaning Often Fails

Many new hosts in Melaka start by doing the cleaning themselves or hiring a part-time domestic helper. While this seems like a way to save money, it often leads to "Review Suicide."

### 1. The Time Pressure Trap
Check-out is at 12 PM. Check-in is at 3 PM. In a busy location like Melaka Raya or Jonker Street, you have exactly 3 hours to:
- Strip and wash all linens and towels
- Sanitize the kitchen and bathrooms
- Mop and vacuum the entire unit
- Restock essentials (toilet paper, soap, coffee)
- Check for any damages or missing items

If you have a 3-bedroom unit at Atlantis or The Shore, doing this solo is nearly impossible. Professional teams work in pairs or trios to ensure every corner is hit within that tight window.

### 2. The "Invisible" Dirt
A professional Airbnb cleaner looks for things a normal cleaner misses:
- Inside the microwave and fridge
- Under the bed (where guests often drop things)
- Ceiling fan blades (which collect Melaka's humid dust quickly)
- The "drain smell" in older Melaka properties that needs specialized treatment

### 3. The Consistency Problem
Consistency is what builds Superhost status. If your cleaning is "great" 9 times but "okay" the 10th time, that 10th guest will leave a 3-star review that drags down your average for months.
