# Airbnb Commission Rate Malaysia 2026: The Secret Costs You’re Not Told

If you’re thinking about listing your property on Airbnb in Malaysia, the first question you probably have is: **"How much does Airbnb take?"**

Most new hosts see the "3%" figure on the website and think that’s the end of it. It’s not. 

Between host fees, guest service fees, and the way the algorithm treats your pricing, the "real" cost of a booking is much higher than 3%. 

If you aren't careful, you could be losing 15% to 20% of your potential revenue before you even pay for cleaning. 

Let's break down the actual Airbnb commission rates in Malaysia for 2026.

---

## 1. The Standard Split: 3% + 14%

Airbnb typically uses a **Split-Fee structure** for individual hosts in Malaysia.

*   **Host Service Fee:** Usually **3%**. This is what Airbnb deducts from your payout. If a guest pays RM100 for a night, you get RM97.
*   **Guest Service Fee:** Usually **under 14.2%**. This is added *on top* of your nightly rate. The guest sees RM114.20 on the checkout page.

**The Trap:** While you only "pay" 3%, your guest is paying 14% more than your nightly rate. This makes your property look more expensive compared to hotels or direct bookings.

## 2. The Professional Model: Simplified Pricing (15%)

If you manage a lot of properties or use certain property management software, Airbnb might force you into **Simplified Pricing**.

*   **Host Service Fee:** **15%** (fixed). 
*   **Guest Service Fee:** **0%**.

In this model, the price you set is the price the guest sees. While it feels like you're paying more (15% vs 3%), it actually improves your conversion rate because guests hate "hidden fees" at checkout. 

**Pro Tip:** Most property managers in Melaka will try to charge you a 20% management fee *on top* of this 15%. That means **35% of your money** is gone before you even see it. This is exactly why we created our Flat Fee model.

## 3. The "Hidden" Costs of Airbnb Malaysia

Beyond the service fees, there are several "leakages" that eat into your ROI:

### The Currency Exchange Gap
Airbnb often processes payments in USD or SGD before converting them to MYR. If your settings aren't optimized, you can lose **1% to 2%** just on the bank's exchange rate spread.

### VAT/Service Tax
In Malaysia, digital services are subject to tax. Airbnb is required to collect and remit tax on their service fees. This doesn't come out of your pocket directly, but it inflates the final price the guest pays, making you less competitive.

### The "Discount" Pressure
The Airbnb algorithm loves discounts. New listing discounts (20%), weekly discounts (10%), and monthly discounts (20%). If you toggle all of these on, your RM300/night property suddenly becomes RM180/night.
