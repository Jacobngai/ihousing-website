# Airbnb Host Requirements Malaysia: Stop Guessing, Start Hosting Legally

Starting an Airbnb in Malaysia isn't just about throwing some IKEA furniture in a room and waiting for the RM to roll in. In 2026, the "wild west" days are over. 

If you don't follow the **Airbnb host requirements in Malaysia**, you risk heavy fines, being banned by your JMB, or worse—losing your insurance coverage when you need it most.

At iHousing, we’ve navigated these rules for 8 years across 700+ properties in Melaka. Here is the direct, no-fluff guide to staying legal and profitable.

---

## 1. The Legal "Must-Haves"

### MOTAC Registration (Ministry of Tourism, Arts and Culture)
Registration with MOTAC is now a federal mandate for all short-term rental (STR) operators. 
- **The Rule:** You must register your unit as a "Private Home Stay."
- **Why it matters:** Without this, you are technically an illegal hotel. If a guest gets injured, your insurance company will use your lack of registration as a reason to reject your claim.

### The JMB/MC "Power Move"
Under a landmark Federal Court ruling, your condo management (JMB/MC) has the legal right to ban Airbnb if the majority of owners vote for it.
- **Before you buy or furnish:** Read the building bylaws. 
- **Look for "Commercial Title":** SOHO, SOVO, and Serviced Apartments are generally safer, but even they aren't immune to house rule changes.

---

## 2. Financials: The Success Tax (LHDN)

Your Airbnb income is taxable business income.
- **Declare to LHDN:** Don't hide this income. The Inland Revenue Board is actively cross-referencing STR data.
- **The "Owner's Advantage":** Almost everything you spend to run your Airbnb—WiFi, electricity, cleaning fees, repairs, and even management fees—is tax-deductible. Keep your receipts.

---

## 3. Property Safety Standards (Non-Negotiable)

Melaka guests in 2026 expect more than just a bed. If you skip these, you'll get 3-star reviews and zero repeat bookings.

### Essential Amenities
- **High-Speed WiFi:** 100Mbps is the bare minimum. Guests will "refund-trap" you if the WiFi is slow.
- **Digital Locks:** Essential for self-check-in. Nobody wants to wait at a lobby for 30 minutes to get a physical key.
- **Air-Con Maintenance:** Melaka is hot. A leaking AC on a Saturday night is a RM200 refund waiting to happen.

### Mandatory Safety Gear
- **Fire Extinguisher:** 2kg dry powder, clearly visible.
- **First Aid Kit:** Stock it with Panadol, plasters, and antiseptic.

## 4. Operational Requirements (The Hard Part)

Being a host is a 24/7 commitment. To hit **85% occupancy**, you need:

1. **Guest Screening:** You need a process to spot "party guests" before they trash your unit. 
2. **1-Hour Response Time:** Airbnb's algorithm rewards speed. If you take 3 hours to reply to an inquiry while you're at dinner, you’ve already lost the booking.
3. **Professional Cleaning:** "DIY cleaning" usually fails after the first month. You need a team that knows how to sanitize for 5-star reviews.

## Savvy Host FAQ

### Can I host in a residential-titled landed house?
Yes, but check with your Residents' Association (RA). In Melaka, landed STRs are popular but require careful neighbor management to avoid noise complaints.

### Is MOTAC registration expensive?
No. The fee is minimal (often under RM100), but the paperwork can be tedious. It’s the "license to operate" that protects your business.

### How do I handle guest damages?
Don't rely solely on Airbnb's "AirCover." You should have specific STR insurance from a Malaysian provider to cover major property damage or public liability.
