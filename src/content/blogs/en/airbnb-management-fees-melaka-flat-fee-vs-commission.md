# Airbnb Management Fees Melaka 2026: Why 20% Commission Is Brutal on Your ROI

If you own a unit at **Silverscape**, **The Shore**, **Parkland Residence**, or anywhere in the Melaka tourist belt, you’ve probably seen the same pitch from property managers:

**“We only charge 20% commission.”**

Sounds harmless. It isn’t.

That 20% comes off your **gross revenue**, not your profit. So when your unit has a great month, your manager gets paid more even though the workload barely changes. That’s a bad deal for owners.

At iHousing, we’ve managed **700+ properties in Melaka since 2017**. We’ve seen how commission-heavy models quietly kill owner ROI. This guide breaks down the real math, the hidden costs, and why more owners are moving to flat-fee management in 2026.

## The Real Cost of Airbnb Management Fees in Melaka

Most Airbnb management companies in Melaka charge between **20% and 25%** of monthly gross revenue.

Here’s what that actually means in Ringgit:

| Monthly Revenue | 20% Commission | 25% Commission | Yearly Cost at 20% |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| RM 3,000 | RM 600 | RM 750 | RM 7,200 |
| RM 5,000 | RM 1,000 | RM 1,250 | RM 12,000 |
| RM 7,000 | RM 1,400 | RM 1,750 | RM 16,800 |
| RM 10,000 | RM 2,000 | RM 2,500 | RM 24,000 |

If your property performs well during weekends, school holidays, or festive periods, commission pricing turns into a **success tax**.

That’s the problem.

---

## Why Flat Fee Beats Commission for Most Serious Owners

A flat-fee model works because the operator’s workload does **not** increase in proportion to your revenue.

If your condo has a stronger month because demand spikes during Hari Raya, Chinese New Year, or a long weekend from Singapore, the manager did not suddenly do 2x the work. But under commission pricing, they often get paid 2x more anyway.

### What a Flat Fee Changes

- **Predictable costs** every month
- **Better ROI forecasting** for owners
- **No penalty for high-performing months**
- **Cleaner owner statements** with less fee volatility

That matters if you treat your property like an investment instead of a hobby.

## The Hidden Costs Owners Miss When Comparing Fees

This is where most owners screw it up: they compare only the fee percentage.

That’s too shallow.

The real question is:

**What result do you get for the fee?**

### 1. Slow Guest Response Costs You Bookings
If inquiries sit unanswered, your conversion rate drops. Fast response times matter on Airbnb and Booking.com. A cheap manager with slow response is not cheap.

### 2. Weak Pricing Strategy Leaves Revenue on the Table
If rates are not adjusted for weekends, events, school holidays, or sudden demand spikes, the owner loses far more than they save on management fees.

### 3. Outsourced Cleaning Creates Review Risk
Bad turnovers lead to 4-star reviews, complaints, and refund requests. Once review quality drops, occupancy gets hit next.

### 4. Single-Platform Dependence Is Lazy
Some managers rely too heavily on Airbnb alone. That’s amateur hour. A serious Melaka property should also capture demand from **Agoda**, **Booking.com**, **Expedia**, and **Agoda Homes**.

### 5. Poor Issue Handling Kills Owner Confidence
Guest lockout. Broken aircond. Missing towels. Water heater problem. If your manager can’t solve those fast, you are not buying peace of mind—you are buying stress.
