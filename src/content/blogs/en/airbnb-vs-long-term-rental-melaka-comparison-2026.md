You've just picked up the keys to your new condo in Melaka. Maybe it's at The Apple, or Silverscape, or a nice landed unit in Klebang. Now comes the big question every owner faces:

*"Do I find a steady tenant for RM1,500 a month and forget about it? Or do I try this Airbnb thing everyone is talking about?"*

We get asked this almost every day. On paper, Airbnb always looks like the winner. Who wouldn't want RM4,000 a month instead of RM1,500? But as local property managers who have handled over 100 units in Melaka, we can tell you: the spreadsheet doesn't tell the whole story.

Let's break down the real numbers, the hidden headaches, and the 5-year outlook for both strategies in the current Melaka market.

## The "Paper" Promise vs. Reality

Let's start with the numbers everyone looks at first.

**Scenario:** A 2-bedroom condo in Melaka Raya (e.g., Silverscape or Imperium).
**Market Value:** ~RM450,000

| Metric | Long-Term Rental (Traditional) | Airbnb (Short-Term Rental) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Gross Monthly Income** | RM1,400 - RM1,600 (Fixed) | RM3,500 - RM5,500 (Variable) |
| **Occupancy Risk** | Low (12-month contract) | High (Seasonal dips in Ramadan/weekdays) |
| **Utility Bills** | Tenant pays | You pay (~RM300-RM600/month) |
| **Cleaning/Maintenance** | Minimal (Tenant handles daily) | Frequent (RM400+/month) |
| **Management Effort** | Low (Collect rent, fix urgent leaks) | High (Daily messaging, check-ins, reviews) |

At first glance, Airbnb seems to generate **2-3x the gross income**. And during peak seasons like Chinese New Year or the December school holidays, we've seen units pull in RM7,000+ in a single month.

But gross income isn't profit.

## The Hidden Costs of Airbnb in Melaka

When you rent long-term, your costs are predictable: Maintenance fee (JMB), quit rent, and assessment.

With Airbnb, you become a hotelier. That means you're paying for:
1.  **Electricity:** Guests love sleeping with the aircon at 16Â°C while under a duvet. Your TNB bill *will* shock you.
2.  **Internet:** High-speed WiFi (TIME or Unifi) is mandatory (RM100+/month).
3.  **Replacements:** Towels get stained, glasses break, and bedsheets need replacing every 6-8 months.
4.  **Cleaning:** You can charge a cleaning fee, but it rarely covers deep cleans or laundry logistics.
5.  **Platform Fees:** Airbnb takes ~3%, and if you use other platforms like Agoda or Booking.com, commissions can hit 15-18%.

### So, what's the real net difference?

After deducting utilities, cleaning, supplies, and platform fees, **a well-managed Airbnb in Melaka typically nets about 30-50% more than long-term rental.**

Is 50% more income worth the effort? That depends on your "Headache Tolerance."

## The "Headache Factor": Tenants vs. Guests

This is where the decision usually gets made.

### Long-Term Rental Headaches
-   **The "Runaway" Tenant:** We've all heard horror stories of tenants leaving in the middle of the night with 3 months of unpaid rent and a trashed apartment.
-   **Wear and Tear:** A tenant living there 365 days a year cooks, showers, and lives in your unit. Over 5 years, the kitchen cabinets and flooring take a beating.
-   **Stagnant Rates:** Once signed, you can't raise rent for a year (or two).

### Airbnb Headaches
-   **The "Party" Guests:** Dealing with noise complaints from neighbors at 2 AM because your guests decided to have a karaoke session.
-   **Constant Communication:** "Where is the key?" "How do I turn on the heater?" "Is the pool open?" You are on call 24/7.
-   **Review Anxiety:** One bad review because the WiFi was slow for 30 minutes can tank your ranking for weeks.

**Our take:** Airbnb units actually stay in *better* condition than long-term rentals. Why? Because they are cleaned professionally 5-10 times a month, and guests rarely cook heavy meals. We catch maintenance issues (like a leaky tap) immediately, rather than discovering a rotted cabinet after a tenant moves out two years later.

## Location Matters: Jonker vs. Klebang vs. Melaka Raya

The "Airbnb vs Long-Term" answer changes depending on where your property is.

### 1. Tourist Hotspots (Melaka Raya, Kota Laksamana)
**Verdict: Airbnb wins.**
Properties like **Silverscape**, **The Apple**, or **Imperium** are built for tourists. They are close to Mahkota Parade and Jonker Street. Long-term tenants often find these areas too congested or noisy. Demand for short-term stays here is consistent year-round.

### 2. Family Areas (Klebang, Bukit Baru)
**Verdict: It's a toss-up.**
Klebang has great seaview condos, but it's a drive from the main tourist spots. While weekends are busy, weekdays can be dead. Unless you have a unique unit (like a direct sea-facing penthouse), long-term rental to working professionals or expats might be more stable.

### 3. Residential Suburbs (Ayer Keroh, Cheng)
**Verdict: Long-Term wins.**
Tourists rarely stay in Ayer Keroh unless they are visiting the zoo or a specific event. These areas are better suited for families working nearby or students (MMU/UTem).

## Summary: Which is Right for You?

**Choose Long-Term Rental if:**
-   You prioritize stability over maximum income.
-   Your property is in a non-tourist residential area.
-   You cannot handle *any* fluctuation in monthly income.

**Choose Airbnb (Short-Term Rental) if:**
-   Your property is in a prime tourist location (Melaka City Center).
-   You want to use the property yourself occasionally (block out dates for your own holidays!).
-   You want to maximize yield and keep the property in "showroom" condition.
-   You are willing to manage it yourself OR hire a professional team.
