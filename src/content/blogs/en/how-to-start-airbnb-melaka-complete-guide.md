So you've got a property in Melaka and you're thinking, "Why not Airbnb it?" Maybe you saw your neighbor making decent monthly income, or you're tired of dealing with long-term tenants who stop paying rent. Whatever brought you here, you're probably wondering: where do I even start?

We've helped over 100 property owners in Melaka launch their Airbnbs, and we've seen it all—the smooth launches and the "I wish I'd known that before" moments. Here's what actually works.

## Step 1: Pick the Right Location (Or Work With What You Have)

**Already own a property?** Skip to the next section. But if you're still shopping, location matters more than fancy renovations.

### The Winners in Melaka

**Jonker Street area:** Highest occupancy rates, especially weekends. Chinese New Year? You can charge double. Downside: Noise from tourists, parking is a nightmare.

**Klebang Beach:** Popular with families and Singapore weekend trips. Slightly lower nightly rates than Jonker, but more consistent bookings during school holidays.

**Melaka Raya:** Business travelers and longer stays. Less sexy than heritage areas, but you get fewer party groups and more respectful guests.

**What actually matters:**
- Walking distance to something (Jonker Walk, beach, A Famosa)
- Parking availability (this is HUGE—guests complain about this constantly)
- Quiet enough at night (check at 11pm on a Saturday, not 3pm on a Tuesday)

We've seen stunning condos in the middle of nowhere sit empty while basic units near Jonker are booked 25+ nights a month.

## Step 2: Set Up Your Space (Don't Overthink It)

You don't need an interior designer or Instagram-worthy decor. You need the basics done properly.

### The Essentials Guests Actually Care About

**Bed & bedding:**
- Good mattress (they'll mention a bad one in reviews)
- Minimum 2 sets of bedding per bed (for quick turnovers)
- Extra pillows and blankets (some like firm, some like soft)

**WiFi:**
- Fast and reliable (get business fiber if possible)
- Password clearly displayed
- We test it monthly—nothing kills reviews faster than "WiFi didn't work"

**Air conditioning:**
- This is Melaka. Not optional.
- Service it quarterly—guests will complain if it's weak
- Remote controls that actually work

**Kitchen/Kitchenette:**
- Basic cookware (even if you think guests won't cook, some will)
- Coffee maker (instant coffee is fine, don't go crazy)
- Rice cooker (Malaysian guests expect this)

**Bathroom:**
- Hot water shower
- Decent water pressure
- Towels (2 per guest minimum)
- Basic toiletries (shampoo, soap—saves you "where's the shampoo?" messages)

### What You Can Skip

- Fancy art (gets damaged or stolen)
- Expensive electronics (same reason)
- Decorative items you care about (someone will break them)

Think hotel room, not your dream home. Functional beats pretty.

## Step 3: Get Your MOTAC License (Yes, You Need It)

Legally, short-term rentals in Malaysia need a MOTAC (Ministry of Tourism) license. We've written a complete guide on [MOTAC requirements](/blog/motac-license-requirements-airbnb-malaysia/), but here's the quick version:

**Requirements:**
- Property must be registered
- Fire safety equipment (extinguisher, smoke detector)
- Public liability insurance
- Business registration (can be under your name)

**Processing time:** 2-4 weeks if your paperwork is complete (8+ weeks if they keep asking for more documents).

**Cost:** RM200-500 for license, plus professional fees if you hire someone to handle it.

**Do you need it from Day 1?** Technically yes. Practically, many hosts apply after their first few bookings. We don't recommend cutting corners, but we also know how it actually happens.

## Step 4: Create Your Listing (First Impressions Matter)

Your listing is your sales page. Boring descriptions = fewer bookings.

### Photos That Get Clicks

**What to shoot:**
- Main bedroom (bright, clean, bed made)
- Living area (wide angle to show space)
- Bathroom (spotless)
- View or balcony (if you have one)
- Neighborhood (show you're near something)

**Phone camera is fine.** Shoot during the day with natural light. No need for a professional photographer—guests want to see the space, not artistic angles.

**Pro tip:** Take a photo of the exact view from your balcony/window. Guests love knowing what they'll wake up to.

### Writing Your Description (Be Specific)

**❌ Generic (skip this):**
> "Cozy apartment in Melaka. Close to attractions. Perfect for families."

**✅ What actually works:**
> "5-minute walk to Jonker Walk night market. Fits 4 guests comfortably (1 queen bed, 1 sofa bed). Free parking, fast WiFi, and a washing machine if you need it. We've hosted 200+ guests—mostly Singapore families and couples."

**Include:**
- Exact walking time to major spots (Google Maps, don't guess)
- Parking situation (free? paid? street? building?)
- Who stays here most often (helps guests self-select)
- Any quirks (3rd-floor walkup, no elevator, etc.)

### Pricing: The Tricky Part

**Research your competition:**
- Search Airbnb for your area
- Filter by similar size/amenities
- Check what's booked vs sitting empty

**Our rough Melaka pricing guide (per night):**
- **Jonker area:** RM180-350 (weekends higher, CNY can hit RM500+)
- **Klebang:** RM150-280
- **Melaka Raya:** RM130-220

**Start 10% lower than you want** to get your first few bookings and reviews. After 5+ five-star reviews, raise it.

**Use dynamic pricing** (Airbnb's Smart Pricing or a tool like PriceLabs) to adjust automatically for weekends, holidays, and events.

## Step 5: Prepare for Your First Guest

You've got the listing up, you've got your first booking. Now what?

### The Week Before Check-In

**Communication:**
- Send a welcome message 3 days before ("Looking forward to hosting you!")
- Include check-in instructions (time, how to get keys, parking details)
- Ask if they need anything specific (crib, extra towels, local recommendations)

**Final check:**
- Clean thoroughly (or hire a cleaner—worth every ringgit)
- Stock consumables (toilet paper, dish soap, coffee, sugar)
- Test everything (AC, WiFi, hot water, door locks)
- Fresh linens on beds

### Check-In Day

**Options for key handoff:**
1. **Meet in person** (time-consuming but you can show them around)
2. **Lockbox/digital lock** (guests enter a code, no coordination needed)
3. **Building security** (if your condo has 24/7 guard)

We recommend digital locks. Costs RM300-800 upfront, saves hours of scheduling.

**What to have ready:**
- Welcome note with WiFi password, emergency contacts, house rules
- Recommendations sheet (best chicken rice, where to get breakfast, taxi numbers)
- House manual (how to use appliances, where circuit breakers are)

## Step 6: The First Month Reality Check

**What usually goes wrong:**
- Guests lock themselves out (get a backup key ready)
- AC remote stops working (have spares)
- Neighbor complains about noise (set clear quiet hours)
- Guest leaves bad review over something fixable (respond professionally, fix it)

**What usually goes right:**
- Positive reviews (most guests are lovely)
- Monthly income beats long-term rental (if occupancy is good)
- You figure out your rhythm (messaging, cleaning, pricing)
