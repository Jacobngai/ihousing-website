# Airbnb Operating Costs Malaysia 2026: Complete Melaka Guide (700+ Properties Data)

Most property investors in Melaka look at two numbers: **The Installment** and **The Gross Revenue**. 

If the revenue is higher than the installment, they think they are making money. 

**This is a dangerous assumption.**

After 8+ years managing 700+ properties in Silverscape, Parkland, and Atlantis, we've seen that "Hidden Leakages" are what determine if an investment is a cash-cow or a money-pit. In 2026, with rising utility costs and service taxes, understanding your operating expenses is more critical than ever.

Here is the breakdown of the real costs of running an Airbnb in Melaka.

---

## 1. The Big Leak: Utilities (Electricity & Water)

Electricity is the single largest variable expense for any Melaka Airbnb.

*   **The Problem:** Guests in Melaka come from the hot sun and immediately set the AC to 16°C. Then they go out for dinner at Jonker Street for 5 hours-**leaving the AC running.**
*   **The Cost:** In a 2-bedroom unit, unmanaged electricity can easily hit **RM400 - RM600 per month**.
*   **The iHousing Fix:** We install energy-saving sensors and smart AC controllers. We also set "Comfort Zones" (22-24°C) that keep guests happy while preventing the compressor from running 24/7.

## 2. Maintenance & Wear and Tear (The Silent ROI Killer)

An Airbnb isn't a long-term rental. It has 10x the "human traffic."

*   **Plumbing:** Melaka's hard water causes mineral buildup in heaters and taps. Expect to service your water heaters every 12 months.
*   **Painting & Scuffs:** Luggage wheels and kids leave marks. You should budget for a professional touch-up every 18 months to maintain your "Cleanliness" rating.
*   **Furniture Replacement:** Even high-quality mattresses sag and sofas stain. Budget **2% of your gross revenue** into a "Sinking Fund" for furniture replacement.

## 3. The Digital Leak: Platform Fees & Hidden Taxes

We covered this in our [Airbnb Commission Guide](/blog/airbnb-commission-rate-malaysia-2026), but it bears repeating.

*   **Service Tax (SST):** Digital services are taxed.
*   **Transaction Fees:** If you aren't using a local payment gateway, you are losing **1.5% to 3%** on every booking due to currency conversion spreads.
*   **WiFi:** Don't get the basic 30Mbps plan. For a 5-star review in 2026, you need 100Mbps+ which costs **RM129 - RM159 per month**.

## 4. Consumables & Hospitality Kits

It's not just shampoo. It's the "forgotten" items:
*   Toilet paper, tissue, garbage bags.
*   Dish soap, laundry detergent (if you have a washer).
*   Coffee, sugar, creamer, and filtered water.
*   **The Cost:** Budget **RM5 - RM8 per booking** for high-quality consumables. Cheap soap leads to bad reviews.

## FAQ: Airbnb Operating Costs Malaysia

### How much does electricity cost for Airbnb in Melaka?
Electricity is the single largest variable expense. Unmanaged 2-bedroom units easily hit RM400-600/month due to guests leaving AC running. With smart AC controllers and energy sensors, iHousing reduces this to RM250-350/month—saving RM150-250 monthly.

### What are the hidden costs of running Airbnb in Malaysia?
Beyond utilities: platform fees (15-20%), cleaning (RM80-120/turnover), maintenance fund (2% of gross revenue), consumables (RM5-8/booking), WiFi (RM129-159/mo for 100Mbps+), and furniture replacement every 2-3 years. Total hidden costs can eat 40-60% of gross revenue for DIY hosts.

### What is the typical profit margin for Melaka Airbnb?
DIY hosts typically see 30-40% net profit margin after all expenses. Professional management with flat-fee models (like iHousing) achieve 60-70% net margins through bulk maintenance pricing, energy optimization, and multi-platform revenue maximization.

### Should I hire Airbnb property management in Melaka?
If your time is worth more than RM30/hour, yes. Self-management takes 20+ hours monthly. Professional management also reduces electricity (smart controls), maintenance costs (wholesale rates), and increases occupancy (multi-platform optimization). The math usually favors professional management for investors with 1+ properties.

### How much should I budget for Airbnb maintenance in Melaka?
Budget 2% of gross annual revenue for a "Sinking Fund" covering furniture replacement, painting touch-ups, and appliance repairs. For a property earning RM6,000/month (RM72K/year), that's RM1,440/year or RM120/month. Also budget separately for water heater servicing every 12 months (RM150-300) and professional repainting every 18 months (RM800-1,500).
